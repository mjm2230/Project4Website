A Brief History of Lao PDR

Laos, officially known as the Lao People's Democratic Republic, is a landlocked country in Southeast Asia bordered by Thailand, Myanmar, China, Vietnam, and Cambodia. Although it has a population less than the population of New York City, Laos is an incredibly diverse country with dozens of different ethnic groups and languages spoken. Formerly a thriving feudal kingdom and then a colony of France, Laos is now governed by an officially communist government. Laos played a key role in the Vietnam War, as much of the Ho Chi Minh trail went through Laos. Almost as many bombs dropped on Europe and Asia in World War II were dropped on Laos during the Vietnam War. Millions of bombs remain unexploded in Laos and pose a great threat to its people. Every year, around 50 bombs explode, killing or injuring mostly children and impoverished farmers.

    • SAIL Program

    To begin my time in Laos, the 5 other Fulbrighters and I completed the SAIL Program, a study abroad program organized by the Center for Lao Studies in San Francisco. During the month-long program, we studied Lao language and Lao history and culture at the Lao-American Collete in Vientiane, Laos. Every weekend, we traveled the country, visiting a rural farming village in Vientiane Province, Pakse, and Si Pan Done.

    Volunteering at the US Embassey

    Throughout my year in Laos, I volunteered at the US Embassey in Vientiane. I taught free, weekly English classes to the public and went on service trips around the country wtih embassey personnel to donate books and supplies to lcoal schools and libraries. Many of my students at the Embassey were monks from local temples.

    Teaching at the National University of Laos

    The main obligation of my Fulbright in Laos was to teach college English at the National University of Laos. I taught Year 1 and Year 2 English as well as composition writing to freshmen and sophomores. Every Friday, my colleague and I held English Converesation Club, where English students at the university could come practice speaking English with native speakers.

    Life in Laos

    Living in Laos was one of the happiest years of my life. Laos is country full of wonderous natural beauty, and its people are warm and friendly. Laos has a very slow pace of life and people value above all else family, fun, and food. Lao food, relying heavily on meat, fresh produce, and sticky rice, is unique and delicious.
